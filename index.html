Your target

What's your nest egg target?

How much you need to save — accounting for the part Social Security will cover once it starts.

Rule of thumb: save about 25× your yearly spending, and you can withdraw ~4%/year without running out.
Social Security only covers part of retirement, starting when you claim it. Waiting longer (up to 70) means bigger checks.

National average benefit (2026, SSA): ~$1,400/mo at 62 · ~$2,000/mo at 67 · ~$2,200/mo at 70. For your real number, check ssa.gov/myaccount.

If you claim at different ages, your self-funded years are calculated in stages — alone until the first check starts, then together once both do.

The plan

How much to put away monthly?

Working backward from your target, your timeline, and an assumed investment return.

Use an inflation-adjusted (real) return, not a raw market average — your spending targets are in today's dollars, so growth needs to be too. ~6-7% real is roughly the long-run historical figure for an all-stock portfolio; many planners now use more conservative forward-looking numbers like 4-5% real, especially for a balanced portfolio or a shorter timeline.
