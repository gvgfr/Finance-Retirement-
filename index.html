Your number

What's your nest egg target?

Two pools: money to live on before Social Security starts, and a long-term portfolio for after.

The 4% rule: save roughly 25× what you plan to spend each year, and you can pull out about 4% annually without running out — a common starting point for a ~30-year retirement. Not a guarantee, just a solid baseline.
If you retire before Social Security starts, your own savings cover that gap alone — we call it the "bridge." This isn't a reason to claim early: waiting longer (up to age 70) permanently raises your monthly check by about 8%/year past your full retirement age, so many people come out ahead by waiting even though it means self-funding more of the bridge.

National average benefit (2026, SSA): ~$1,400/mo at 62 · ~$2,000/mo at 67 · ~$2,200/mo at 70. For your real number, check ssa.gov/myaccount.

If you claim at different ages, the bridge is calculated in stages — alone until the first check starts, then together once both do.
